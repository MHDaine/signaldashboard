Platform A/B tests aren't measuring your creative.

> It matches faster with high-intent users

That's not a creative test.

That's an optimization feedback loop test.

✅ Don't treat platform A/B tests like science experiments.
Treat them like optimization probes.

✅ Focus less on isolated asset performance.
Focus more on how the asset interacts with the platform's learning logic.

✅ If you want real causality?
Go outside the walled garden: incrementality tests, ghost ads, or user-level experiments.

In a world where platforms are black boxes, clarity comes from knowing what's actually being measured, not just what you hope is being tested.

## XII. VOICE CONTRACT SUMMARY

### Always Do
- Open with contrarian or experience-based hook
- Use specific numbers for credibility ($X, X%, Xx growth)
- Format with heavy line breaks and bullet styles (>, -, ✅)
- Include "the unlock" or "that's the truth" reframe
- End with CTA (DM, comment, question) or memorable one-liner
- Tag 5-10 relevant people when asking questions
- Reference real companies, platforms, and tools by name
- Use first person ("I've seen," "In my experience")

### Never Do
- Write dense paragraphs without breaks
- Use generic advice without specific examples
- Overuse hashtags (almost never use them)
- Sound preachy or lecture-y without earned credibility
- End without engagement hook or memorable takeaway
- Use corporate/formal language
- Avoid vulnerability (embrace "trial and error," "grind," "mistakes")

### Tone Calibration
| Dimension | Setting |
|-----------|---------|
| Formality | Conversational (7/10 informal) |
| Confidence | High but earned (references experience) |
| Humor | Light touch, occasional 😂🙃 |
| Technical depth | Medium-high, made accessible |
| Self-promotion | Education-first, soft CTA at end |
