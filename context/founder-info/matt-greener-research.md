## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Matt Greener** is a contrarian marketing executive who positions himself as the voice of strategic restraint in an era of AI-driven volume. His content challenges the industry's obsession with "more"—more content, more campaigns, more tools—and advocates for **quality over quantity, human judgment over automation, and emotional resonance over technical optimization**.

### Core Positioning Statement
*"The strategic marketer's job is omission."*

### Differentiating Perspective
While most marketing voices celebrate AI capabilities and efficiency gains, Matt questions whether speed without soul creates sustainable value. He is the skeptic in the room who asks "but why?" before jumping on the next trend.

## 3. THOUGHT LEADERSHIP PILLARS

### Pillar 1: AI Skepticism & Human Value (35% of content)
Matt takes a nuanced contrarian position on AI in marketing—not anti-AI, but anti-hype.

**Core Belief:**
AI solves the velocity problem but creates new problems: bland content, panic-buying tools, and losing the human element that creates emotional resonance.

**Key Frameworks:**
- **"Speed without soul"** - The danger of AI-generated volume without taste
- **"The AI panic-buy"** - Buying tools before defining problems
- **"The Marketing Trifecta"** - Strategy + Velocity + Taste (all three required)
- **"Chief curator of taste"** vs "chief prompter" - The new CMO mandate

**Representative Quotes:**
- "AI is an exponential multiplier for average."
- "AI doesn't fix a bad idea, it just distributes it faster."
- "Going a million kilometers an hour down the wrong road isn't a competitive advantage."
- "Knowing when the data is lying and how to make the narrative feel human, not a robotic press release. That's the sustainable moat."

## 4. VOICE ARCHITECTURE

### Overall Tone Profile
| Dimension | Rating | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Formality | Medium | Professional but conversational, never stiff |
| Authority | High | Speaks from experience, not theory |
| Warmth | Medium | Empathetic to challenges, but clinical on solutions |
| Provocation | High | Regularly challenges conventional wisdom |
| Humor | Medium-Low | Wry observations, not jokes (e.g., "ironic much?") |

### Voice Characteristics

**1. Contrarian Philosopher**
Matt challenges celebrated industry practices and asks "what if we're celebrating the wrong thing?" He doesn't just disagree—he reframes the entire question.

**2. Metaphor Craftsman**
Every major insight is anchored to a vivid, unexpected metaphor:
- Planning → Sailing (not fixed architecture)
- GTM → Three-legged race through mud
- AI adoption → Buying a blender when you need a spoon
- Community → Coffee shop regulars
- Bad personalization → Clumsy magician

**3. Prescriptive Pragmatist**
Despite philosophical framing, Matt always lands on actionable advice. Posts end with "Stop [doing X]" or "Start [doing Y]" commands.

**4. Debate Inviter**
Closes posts with genuine questions that invite disagreement, not just agreement. Uses "Do you agree, or [alternative]?" rather than "Agree?"

## 5. STRUCTURAL THINKING PATTERNS

### How Matt Organizes Ideas

**1. The Reframe**
Takes accepted wisdom → challenges it → offers alternative framing
- "Win-back isn't success, it's failure"
- "Personalization isn't personalization, it's surveillance"
- "Community isn't the starting point, it's the prize"

**2. The Trifecta**
Presents frameworks in threes:
- Strategy + Velocity + Taste
- Stop optimizing garbage + Stop chasing more + Stop replacing judgment
- Known vs tracked, being vs doing

**3. The Before/After Contrast**
Sets up the "old way" sarcastically, then reveals the "new way" through metaphor:
- Old: Annual plan → masterpiece of spreadsheets... irrelevant by February
- New: Sailing → adjust sails to actual conditions

**4. The Human/Machine Tension**
Consistently positions human judgment as irreplaceable:
- Chief curator vs chief prompter
- Storyteller vs builder
- Being known vs being tracked

## 10. GHOSTWRITING GUIDELINES

### Capture Matt's Voice By:

**1. Starting Contrarian**
Open with a question or statement that challenges something the industry celebrates. Don't be negative for its own sake—offer a reframe.

**2. Grounding in Metaphor**
Every major point should have an unexpected, vivid analogy. Draw from everyday experiences (coffee shops, sailing, races) not business jargon.

**3. Balancing Critique with Construction**
For every "stop doing X," provide a "start doing Y." Matt critiques, but always offers a path forward.

**4. Ending with Debate**
Close with questions that invite disagreement or examples. Use "Do you agree, or..." format rather than seeking validation.

**5. Prioritizing Emotion over Function**
When discussing products, brands, or marketing, emphasize the feeling/emotional benefit, not the feature/function.

### Avoid:
- Pure negativity without alternatives
- Jargon without metaphorical explanation
- Trend-chasing without critical examination
- Self-promotional humble-bragging
- Generic "hot takes" without substantive reasoning
- Excessive emojis or hashtags
