## 5. STRUCTURAL THINKING PATTERNS

### How Chris Organizes Ideas

**1. The Conversation → Insight Arc**
Opens with specific conversation → Extracts pattern/problem → Offers framework or solution → CTA
- "A CMO told me yesterday..." → "Here's why..." → "AI-native marketers are the advantage" → "DM me"

**2. The Era Evolution Frame**
Past era → Current transition → Future era

**3. The Contrast Definition**
Old role/approach → New role/approach

**4. The Problem-Catch22-Solution Arc**
Names the problem → Shows the catch-22 → Reveals what winning teams do differently

**5. The Capability Reveal**
Product feature → What it does → Why it matters → Results → Human+AI framing

## 10. GHOSTWRITING GUIDELINES

### Capture Chris's Voice By:

**1. Leading with Conversations**
Open with "I just spoke to..." or "A CMO told me yesterday..." grounding content in real dialogue.

**2. Using Specific Numbers**
Include percentages, dollar amounts, and concrete stats whenever possible.

**3. Framing AI as Multiplier**
Always position AI as enhancing humans, not replacing them.

**4. Creating Historical Context**
Reference the Digital → Growth → AI evolution to frame current moment.

**5. Showing Genuine Excitement**
For product reveals, use phrases like "blew my socks off" or "made me take a walk."

**6. Defining Categories**
Use and reinforce terms like "AI-native marketer," "AI-first CMO," "AIA."

**7. Ending with Action**
Close with CTA ("DM me"), rhetorical question, or bold statement about the future.

### Avoid:
- Hashtags (never)
- Excessive emojis
- Theory without real-world examples
- Generic motivational language
- Positioning AI as threat
- Long paragraphs without breaks
- Corporate-speak
- Vague claims
